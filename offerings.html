/ F1 · Cells & Infra

Sovereign Cell Starter

Combines the Sovereign Node Activation Sprint with a simple Homestead/Citadel Upgrade v0. One package to take a person or small group from “plugged into synthetic systems” to “running their own stack with a physical resilience plan.”

Who this is for

Individuals, couples, or 3–15 person cells who want a working sovereign stack (Bitcoin, comms, backups, privacy) plus a realistic path to food/water/energy resilience, even if they are renting or only planning a future site.

Inputs

  • Current device list (laptop/phone/server), accounts, and wallets (if any).
  • Basic threat model: jurisdictions, income sources, dependence points, risk tolerance.
  • Current living situation (apartment vs land, city vs rural, travel vs fixed base) and rough budget.

Process

  • Session 1 — Mapping & threat model: inventory devices, accounts, exposure; define priorities (capital preservation, censorship resistance, privacy, logistics, care).
  • Session 2 — Sovereign Node Activation Sprint: design and deploy your Bitcoin + Lightning + privacy stack (wallet architecture, node/client choices, backups, basic comms and storage hygiene).
  • Session 3 — Homestead/Citadel Upgrade v0: translate your context into a three-phase plan for water, food, energy, and basic defense (for current home and/or target site).

Outputs & artifacts

  • A live, self-custodied Bitcoin + Lightning stack under your control (no custodial chokepoints), with documented backup/restore and simple runbooks.
  • A SovStack baseline for comms, storage, and privacy (tools selected to match your threat model, not random app lists).
  • A Homestead/Citadel v0 plan: three-phase upgrades for food, water, energy, and basic site security (with ballpark costs and hardware suggestions).
  • A concise PDF runbook and a 30-day checklist so you can extend and maintain the stack without hand-holding.

When you contact, mention “F1 · Sovereign Cell Starter” and include your jurisdiction, living context, and whether you are personal, family, or small business.

/ F2 · BTC & Markets

Bitcoin Commerce Escape Stack

Combines Fiat Chokepoint Escape (FCE) with Lightning Liquidity & Routing as a Service. This is for businesses and independent operators who want to transact in Bitcoin without becoming a compliance honeypot or a toy “crypto payment” side quest.

Who this is for

Merchants, small enterprises, and service providers who earn in fiat, want to shift to Bitcoin flows, and need real operations (not just a tip jar) without blowing up staff, accounting, or risk.

Inputs

  • Simple revenue snapshot: what you sell, to whom, where, and in which currencies.
  • Existing payment stack (gateways, banks, processors, invoicing tools).
  • Jurisdictions, compliance constraints, and tolerance for visibility vs privacy.

Process

  • Stage 1 — Chokepoint audit: map banks, processors, platform dependencies, and “single kill switches” across the business.
  • Stage 2 — Bitcoin commerce design: specify where Bitcoin enters (POS, invoice, web checkout), how you price, handle refunds, and integrate accounting.
  • Stage 3 — Implementation: deploy BTCPay/Lightning/eCash stack, route flows, set staff permissions, define fallback paths.
  • Stage 4 — Lightning operations: manage inbound/outbound liquidity, fees, routing policy, and uptime for a defined ramp-up period.

Outputs & artifacts

  • A working Bitcoin checkout and invoicing flow (online/offline) that you control end-to-end with minimal custodial dependence.
  • A short “Fiat Chokepoint Exposure” report: before/after view of how much of your business still depends on banks and platforms, and how to keep reducing it.
  • A Lightning operations runbook (how to monitor channels, when to rebalance, what to watch for).
  • Simple staff-level SOPs for handling BTC payments, refunds, and edge cases without panicking.

When you contact, mention “F2 · Bitcoin Commerce Escape Stack” and include your business type, countries of operation, and current annual volume range.

/ F3 · Law, Ritual & Advisory

Sovereign Law & Curriculum Program

Anchors your stack in a Sovereign Codex / Covenant and trains people through a Sovereign Curriculum Bootcamp, with optional Sovereign Advisory for high-net-worth and serious node-builders.

Who this is for

Individuals, families, funds, or cells who want their own operating law (not ideology or religion), with clear covenants, collapse/succession plans, and a curriculum to train others into it.

Inputs

  • Existing documents: wills, operating agreements, NDAs, community rules, or nothing but notes.
  • Core non-negotiables around property, family, capital, risk, and conflict.
  • Size and composition of the group (personal, family, fund, company, or mixed cell).

Process

  • Phase 1 — Extraction: structured interviews to surface real values, red lines, and current vulnerabilities (legal, relational, economic).
  • Phase 2 — Codex / Covenant design: draft a Sovereign Codex and simple covenants that define ownership, decision-making, conflict handling, and collapse/succession rules.
  • Phase 3 — Curriculum build: turn the codex into a minimal curriculum: short explanations, practices, checklists, and rites that can be taught and repeated.
  • Phase 4 — Bootcamp: run a 4-week cohort (remote or hybrid) for the core group covering law-core, Bitcoin basics, privacy, and physical resilience as one system.
  • Phase 5 — Advisory (optional): periodic check-ins for high-net-worth or complex nodes to adjust the codex and structure as assets and risk change.

Outputs & artifacts

  • A concise 1-page law-core and a longer Sovereign Codex that can be read, enacted, and updated without priesthood.
  • Covenant templates for property, roles, participation, and exit, including collapse and succession clauses.
  • A curriculum kit (session outlines, readings, exercises, rites) so you can re-run Bootcamp-style training without external help.
  • For advisory clients: a governance and risk runbook that ties codex, capital, and real-world operations together.

When you contact, mention “F3 · Sovereign Law & Curriculum Program” and include whether you are an individual, family, fund, business, or mixed node.
